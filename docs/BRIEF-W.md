# Addendum brief — Study W: who writes the memo? (agent-maintained extraction, the shipped configuration)

**Pre-registration, committed before any scored W run.** Studies T
and V validated the session-notes memo with **oracle extraction**:
the harness knew what to record and recorded it perfectly — a limit
BRIEF-T disclosed as "an upper bound for the memo pattern." What
shipped in Replicator v3.183.0 delegates that judgment to the agent
via an `update_session_notes` tool and a prompt rule, **with a
32-message history window also present**. That is the largest gap
left between what is measured and what is running, and it carries a
specific hypothesis with the series' signature failure shape: within
the window, callbacks succeed via history whether or not the agent
ever writes the memo, so a lazy agent's empty memo is invisible from
task success — until the declaration scrolls out of the window and
the safety net silently isn't there.

## The shipped artifacts (tested VERBATIM; ported from slx-replicator v3.183.0, commit 3c714f4)

- **Tool** `update_session_notes`, full-replace semantics, description
  verbatim: *"Replace the session-notes memo: the durable list of
  declared facts, standing rules, and goals for THIS editing session.
  Call it when the user declares something later edits must honor — a
  fact or value to reuse (a name, a codename), a standing rule ('every
  new text atom gets textStyle body-md'), or a goal that should steer
  later work ('keep every section focused on X') — or retracts one.
  Send the COMPLETE updated list every time; it replaces the previous
  memo. The memo is shown to you at every future turn and outlives the
  visible conversation window, and it is the measured carrier for
  qualitative goals (restate a memo goal in your own words before a
  goal-directed rewrite)."* Input schema: `{ notes: [{ kind:
  "fact"|"rule"|"goal", text: string }] }`.
- **Normalization** `normalizeSessionNotes` ported verbatim (drops
  invalid entries, trims, caps 20 notes / 300 chars, never throws).
- **Prompt rule** `SESSION_NOTES_PROMPT_RULE` verbatim, appended to
  the system prompt.
- **Memo rendering** `formatSessionNotesBlock` verbatim (the
  "## Session notes (app-maintained memo)" section with
  Facts / Standing rules / Goals groups), injected into the system
  prompt each turn.
- **History window**: keep the LAST 32 messages
  (`messages.slice(-32)`), exactly as shipped.

Ports are unit-tested character-identical against the replicator
source. Bench patching prompts (condition F + view rules + session
rules) are unchanged from Studies T/S; only the memo machinery is
the shipped artifact. Disclosed: Replicator's chat prompts differ
from the bench's patch prompts — W tests the shipped *memo
mechanism* under the bench's measured editing protocol, not
Replicator's full prompt stack.

## Corpus: 36-step callback sessions that cross the window

New `corpus/sessions-callback-long.json`, seed **20260717**, 12
sessions (7 l, 5 xl) × 36 steps, generated by the Study T callback
generator extended to the Study S horizon, with a fixed pre-registered
schedule (kinds follow the standard 5-cycle):

- step 1 (set-attribute) declares **F1**: campaign codename X1
- step 3 (remove) declares the **RULE**: every new text atom gets
  textStyle "small-caps"
- step 4 (insert) — rule-governed callback, within window
- step 6 (set-attribute) **RETRACTS F1**: "Correction: the campaign
  codename is now X2 (not X1)."
- step 7 (set-name) — fact callback F1 → expects **X2**, within window
- step 21 (set-attribute) declares **F2**: sponsor codename Y
- step 24 (insert) — rule-governed callback, post-truncation of the
  step-3 rule
- step 27 (set-name) — fact callback F2, declaration still in window
  (control)
- step 32 (set-name) — fact callback F1 → X2, post-truncation AND
  post-retraction (the money cell)
- step 34 (insert) — rule-governed callback, post-truncation

Six callback cells per session (72 per arm-model), three declaring
riders plus one retraction rider. All other steps are ordinary
self-contained edits. Codenames X1, X2, Y from the digit-suffixed
pool (tree-disjoint); no-leakage validation as in T (value absent
from every pre-step tree and every callback instruction).
"Post-truncation" placement is designed against the no-tool-call
message ledger; because agent tool calls add messages and shift
truncation earlier, the runner records each declaring message's
ACTUAL window membership at every request, and the analysis
classifies by the recorded membership, not the design intent.

## Arms (3)

- **W-oracle** — the measured ceiling: stateless + the two worked
  examples + a HARNESS-maintained perfect memo (every declarable,
  retractions applied), rendered with the shipped
  `formatSessionNotesBlock`. Study T's mechanism re-based onto the
  shipped rendering.
- **W-agent** — pure extraction test: stateless + worked examples +
  the shipped tool, prompt rule, and block. The agent is the only
  writer of the memo, which persists across steps app-side.
- **W-agent-history** — the shipped configuration: last-32-message
  window history + the shipped tool, prompt rule, and block. No
  worked examples (history supplies precedent, per Study P).

Editor models: `anthropic/claude-sonnet-4.5`,
`google/gemini-3.5-flash`, and — the series' first data on the tier
Replicator actually runs — `anthropic/claude-opus-4.8`.
12 × 36 × 3 × 3 = **3,888 step records**.

## Grading

Callback and per-step grading unchanged from Studies T/S
(deterministic, per-step ground truth from the model's own pre-step
state; retraction callbacks expect the final value X2). Added
**memo-fidelity metrics**, all deterministic because declarables are
planted:

- **recall**: each active declarable (X2, the rule, Y) present in the
  memo at session end (substring on the planted value / rule token);
- **retraction handling**: X1 ABSENT from the memo at session end;
- **noise**: memo notes matching no declarable (mean count);
- **replace-integrity**: tool calls that dropped a previously-recorded
  still-active declarable (count of drop events);
- **timeliness**: first step at which each declarable appears, minus
  its declaring step.

## Pre-registered hypotheses

- **W-H1 (extraction works when the memo is load-bearing):** W-agent
  callback success is statistically indistinguishable from W-oracle
  per model (McNemar over shared (session, step) keys, p > 0.05) AND
  end-of-session recall ≥ 90% of active declarables. This is the
  de-risking gate for v3.183.0's mechanism in its pure form.
- **W-H2 (redundancy breeds laziness — the primary question):** in
  W-agent-history, POST-TRUNCATION callback success (by recorded
  window membership) vs W-oracle's same cells. The laziness
  prediction: failures concentrate post-truncation and co-occur with
  missing-memo recall, while within-window cells stay clean. The
  **gate**: post-truncation parity with W-oracle (p > 0.05 per
  model). Passing = the shipped config is de-risked end to end;
  failing = the 32-message protection is theater past the window, and
  the fix (a record-before-answering nudge, or app-side candidate
  appending) becomes a measured follow-up.
- **W-H3 (tier calibration, descriptive):** Opus reported on every
  arm and metric, no gate — the series' first evidence on whether the
  recipe advice transfers to the tier it ships on.
- **Secondary:** retraction handling (X1 gone, X2 honored at step 32),
  replace-integrity drop events, per-model tool-call cadence (how
  often each model actually calls the tool).

## Decision rule

Whatever the table says publishes as found; Replicator's session-notes
docs and the barkup docs' memo pattern gain the measured statement
about WHO can write the memo, with the failure anatomy stated
honestly.

## Protocol

As Studies T/S: `maxOutputTokens: 60000`, temperature 0, ≤3
correction rounds per step, session as the resume unit, resumable
JSONL (`results/raw/studyw-<model>.jsonl`), `cacheReadTokens`
recorded, cache audit re-run, empty-reply marker rule (fff5692),
mechanical-failure rules as in Study H. Tool-loop steps allow up to
4 model iterations (tool call + patch); the history ledger includes
tool-call and tool-result messages, constructed defensively against
the Study G response.messages footgun with a loud runtime assertion.
Memo snapshots are recorded per step in the record detail so every
fidelity metric is re-computable offline. **Expected spend $80–140**
(the Opus history arm dominates); abort past $200.

Honesty rules unchanged: this brief, the verbatim ports with their
character-identity tests, the corpus with its schedule and
validations, and all three arm implementations are committed before
the first scored call; results publish whatever they show.
