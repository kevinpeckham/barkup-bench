# Addendum brief — Study S: long sessions (where do the two surviving recipes diverge?)

**Pre-registration, committed before any scored S run.** Every
session finding in this series was measured at 12 edits. At that
horizon the two surviving recipes tie: **keep full history plus a
fresh minimal view each turn** (K-view, Study K's champion) and
**keep no history at all plus two worked examples in the system
prompt** (P-system, Study P's champion; gate passed on both models).
But the recipes scale in opposite directions. The history recipe's
context grows every turn — Study K's rewrite arm hit a reproduced
200k context ceiling at step 11, and even K-view's history
accumulates a view plus a patch per step — while the stateless
recipe costs a flat ~2k tokens per step forever. Study S runs both
recipes through 36-edit sessions to find the divergence: does
statelessness hold when three times as many edits accumulate and
interact, and does the history arm degrade, hit mechanical
ceilings, or merely cost more?

## Arms (pre-registered exactly; prompts unchanged from K/P)

- **S-view** — the K-view policy verbatim (`view` in the session
  runner): one conversation per session, full history, fresh minimal
  focused view attached to every step. System prompt: Study K's
  `VIEW_SYSTEM`, unchanged.
- **S-system** — the P-system policy verbatim (`cannedSys`): every
  step a fresh single-turn conversation, minimal view, the two
  unit-tested worked examples embedded in the system prompt. System
  prompt: Study P's `STATELESS_SYSTEM_EXAMPLES`, unchanged.

No new prompts, no new interventions: Study S is purely a horizon
extension of two already-registered recipes. Records carry the
condition ids `S-view` and `S-system` (same policies, new horizon —
kept distinct from the K/P records so no cross-study analysis can
accidentally pool 12-step and 36-step cells).

## Corpus

New `corpus/sessions-long.json`, committed before any scored call:
seed **20260712**, 5 trees per bucket (l ~150 nodes, xl ~300 nodes)
= **10 sessions × 36 steps**, generated by the Study K session
generator with the step count raised from 12 to 36 (same five-kind
edit cycle, same 50% reference-back rule, same placeholder
mechanism, `validateSession` clean on every task). The 12-step
generator's output is untouched — `corpus/sessions.json` must
regenerate byte-identical after the change.

## Grading

Identical to Studies K/M/O/P: per-step ground truth computed from
the model's own pre-step state; a step with no valid artifact leaves
the tree unchanged; exploratory end-state grade on the final tree
(`equalModuloNewIds` against the generator's expected final).
Mechanical ceilings (context exhaustion) record the step and the
session remainder as mechanical failures, exactly as BRIEF-K.

## Models and cells

`anthropic/claude-sonnet-4.5` and `google/gemini-3.5-flash`,
temperature 0. 10 sessions × 36 steps × 2 arms × 2 models =
**1,440 step records**.

## Pre-registered hypotheses

- **S-H1 (the stateless recipe holds — primary, the gate):**
  S-system at 36 steps shows no late-session collapse and stays at
  parity with S-view. The **gate**, per model: (a) S-system's
  last-third per-step success (steps 25–36) is within 10 points of
  its own first-third (steps 1–12), (b) S-system vs S-view paired
  McNemar over the 360 shared (session, step) keys has p > 0.05,
  and (c) S-system's end-state count is within 2 sessions of
  S-view's. All three on both models = gate passes.
- **S-H2 (cost divergence, measured):** S-view's per-step input
  grows roughly linearly with step index while S-system stays flat;
  prediction: S-view's mean input tokens per session ≥ 3× S-system's.
  Reported regardless of outcome.
- **S-H3 (does history itself crack? — exploratory):** K-view was
  flat at 12 steps; 36 steps is its first long-horizon test.
  Late-session S-view degradation, mechanical ceilings, or output
  bloat are reported descriptively, plus the placement-class
  (insert/move) breakdown from Studies M/O/P.

## Interpretation table (pre-registered)

| S-system gate | S-view late behavior | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| passes | flat | Both recipes hold; stateless wins on cost — it becomes the default long-session guidance |
| passes | degrades | Statelessness is not just cheaper but MORE robust at horizon; history's teaching is superseded by canned examples |
| fails (late collapse) | flat | Teaching decays with distance-from-example; history recipe is the long-session guidance, worked examples capped at short sessions |
| fails | degrades | Neither recipe survives 36 edits; long sessions need checkpointing/decomposition — a new study |

Partial movement short of the gate publishes as partial, exactly as
Studies O and P did.

## Decision rule

If the gate passes, barkup's session guidance gains a horizon
statement: the worked-examples stateless recipe is measured to 36
edits at flat cost, and the docs say so. If S-system fails late, the
guidance reverts to keep-history for long sessions with the measured
crossover, and the worked-examples recipe carries an honest horizon
caveat. Either way the cost curves publish.

## Protocol

Identical to Studies K/M/O/P: `maxOutputTokens: 60000`, temperature
0, 1 attempt + ≤3 correction rounds per step, session as the resume
unit, resumable JSONL (`results/raw/studys-<model>.jsonl`),
`cacheReadTokens` recorded, mechanical-failure rules as in Study H,
cache audit re-run over the new records. **Expected spend $30–60**
(the S-view history arm dominates); abort past $100.

Honesty rules unchanged: this brief, the corpus, and the runner
change (a step-count parameter and a condition-id override, no
behavior change to any registered policy) are committed before the
first scored call; results publish whatever they show.
